WHAT WE DO the LAW & US FOOD DONORS FOOD RECIPIENTS EDUCATION

We rescue surplus food that would otherwise have gone to landfill

SA Harvest collects quality surplus food in refrigerated vehicles from a variety of sources and delivers it, direct, without delay and free of charge, to a variety of organisations that feed food vulnerable people on a daily basis.

Even though we work closely with any organisation that rescues food and fights to reduce waste, one of our most important points of difference is that, because our business model enables us to deliver PERISHABLE as well as NON-PERISHABLE food, our recipient organisations get a sufficient variety of food-types enabling them to make full and nourishing meals for their people.

The work we do has a four fold impact

Quality food for organisations that feed the food-vulnerable – the food that we deliver is nutritionally diverse and enables our recipient organisations to provide wholesome and nutritious meals to the people they support.

Savings for charities – our food deliveries to charitable organisations enable them to redirect vital funding into education, rehabilitation and other programmes. Charities are also able to save money on the transport costs they would have incurred if they had to do their own pick-ups from food donors.

Helping the environment – When good food goes to waste it decays in landfill and creates harmful methane gases that damage our atmosphere. By ensuring that good food is eaten rather than going to landfill, SA HARVEST is helping protect our environment by preventing tons of greenhouse gas emissions from polluting the atmosphere.

Savings for food donors – our food donors benefit from reduced expenditure associated with the disposal of excess food, reduced food storage costs and the satisfaction of knowing they are making a valuable contribution to those in need in their local community.

We rescue food from a wide range of sources

including fruit and vegetable markets, supermarkets, hotels, wholesalers, farmers, stadiums, corporate events, corporate kitchens, catering companies, shopping centres, delis, cafes, restaurants, film and TV shoots, boardrooms and more.

If you are one of the above and have surplus food or are holding an event near us and require a food collection, Contact Us right away.

We deliver fresh, nourishing food for free to organisations that feed food-vulnerable people on a daily basis.

These organisations include homeless shelters, feeding schemes, religious institutions, youth groups, schools, rehabilitation centres, old-age homes, schools any other organisation that feeds food-vulnerable people on a daily basis

If you are one such organisation or you know of an organisation that could benefit from receiving free food deliveries, we'd love to hear from you. Please Contact Us right away.

EDUCATION, both formal and informal, is a critical part of what SA HARVEST does. Through education we increase community connectedness, provide the skills for real jobs and improve self-worth and confidence.

Some of our key educational programmes are:

  • Nutrition Education Sustenance Training (NEST) – Nutrition Education Sustenance Training (NEST) – teaching people of all ages the skills to nurture themselves through healthier eating choices and food preparation methods. Based on the OzHarvest NEST programme, we will provide this offering to our beneficiary organisations.
  • Nourish – providing young people with the necessary training to gain employment in the hospitality industry. Working with vulnerable youth to provide a pathway to employment. Also based on the successful OzHarvest programme, we aim for our course to become accredited according to the South African National Qualifications Framework (NQF).

  • SA Harvest rescues food good quality food in refrigerated trucks and delivers it DIRECTLY to its beneficiary organisations. This is completely legal in South Africa. Our risk is the same as ANY OTHER FOOD VENDOR e.g. supermarket, restaurant, manufacturer etc.

    Consequently, our risk, as with any other food vendor, is mitigated by our adherence to the law, to hygienic processes and the overall professionalism of our operation.


  • SA HARVEST’s five basic actions in complying are:
    • Total compliance with the Foodstuffs, Cosmetics and Disinfectants Act (1972) and the Consumer Protection Act (2008).
    • Following the guidelines of HACCP regulations and continuing with the process of implementing HACCP audits at all our sites.
    • Adhering to local municipal requirements at all our sites.
    • Ensuring the implementation of the strictest cleanliness and general hygiene processes throughout our operation.
    • Driver and recipient training in these processes
